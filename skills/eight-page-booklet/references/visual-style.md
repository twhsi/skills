# Eight Page Booklet Visual Style

Use the real folded-booklet photograph at `assets/eight-page-booklet-paper-photo.jpeg` as the primary proof image. Preserve its documentary character; do not replace it with a synthetic mockup when publishing the skill.

## Visual Thesis

Show a paper thinking object: quiet white paper, black structural lines, visible folds, generous margins, and a human-scale object that can be printed, held, cut, written on, and returned to the desk.

## Shared twhsi.com Language

Use the public BIRD and twhsi.com visual system when a colored digital surface is needed:

- ink: `#201d1e`
- paper: `#f4f0e4`
- white paper: `#fffdf5`
- forest: `#113f31`
- yellow: `#f4dc4f`
- pink: `#de5b86`
- green: `#68a857`
- orange: `#ef6f3b`

Use Rockwell or another slab serif for large English display words. Use PingFang TC or Noto Sans TC for Chinese and body copy. Use thick black rules, square cards, restrained shadows, and one accent color at a time.

## Paper Rules

- Prefer black-and-white for the actual booklet pages.
- Keep an inset of roughly 6 mm around every folded page.
- Let fold paths pass through blank paper rather than decorative frames.
- Use one clear title, three to five concise lines, and writable space.
- Treat real fold, cut, and print evidence as more authoritative than screen polish.

## Public Preview Rules

- Place the portrait booklet photo inside a forest, paper, or ink-colored field.
- Keep the full physical object visible; do not crop away its standing form or fold.
- Pair the photo with a short functional promise such as “One A4 sheet. Eight pages. One thinking route.”
- Avoid fake paper textures, glossy product rendering, excessive gradients, and decorative AI motifs.
- Link the preview directly to the skill source or installation command.

## Relationship To BIRD

Use the booklet as the paper endpoint of a BIRD route:

`Permanent Note → Book Address → Route → BookletManifest → Folded Booklet`

Show structure first. Color identifies layers; it does not replace hierarchy, addresses, or source evidence.
